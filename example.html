Idi Amin Dada

The Controversial Leader of Uganda (1971–1979)

Biography

Idi Amin Dada (1925–2003) was a Ugandan military officer who ruled Uganda as a dictator from 1971 to 1979. Known for his brutal regime, he was responsible for the deaths of an estimated 100,000 to 500,000 people.

"There is freedom of speech, but I cannot guarantee freedom after speech."
— Idi Amin

Key Facts

  • 1925 - Born in Koboko, Uganda.
  • 1971 - Seizes power in a military coup.
  • 1972 - Expels Asians from Uganda, leading to economic collapse.
  • 1976 - Allows hijacked Air France plane to land in Entebbe (Entebbe Raid).
  • 1979 - Overthrown by Tanzanian forces and Ugandan exiles.
  • 2003 - Dies in exile in Saudi Arabia.
